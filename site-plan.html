WDD 231 - Frontend Web Development I

Site Name

Proposed Name: ArtisanSourdough.org

Reason for Selection: This domain name clearly communicates the platform's core premise: a dedicated space for artisan home baking focusing strictly on natural wild yeast fermentation. It targets hobbyist bakers who respect traditional culinary arts, and the .org framework signals a resource-heavy, community-driven educational space rather than an aggressively commercial e-commerce storefront.

Site Purpose

The purpose of the Artisan Sourdough Hub is to serve as an interactive instructional manual and mathematical scaling utility for home bakers seeking to master wild yeast fermentation. The site addresses the complex physics and biochemistry of bread-making by offering clear instructional frameworks, an automated interactive hydration scaling utility calculator, and an integrated diagnostic ledger to isolate and fix fermentation issues.

Target Audience User Scenarios

The site design and content strategy are directed by the following real-world scenarios posed by target site visitors:

  • Scenario 1: "My sourdough loaf is coming out of the oven dense, gummy, and completely flat. How can I cross-reference my specific crumb structure profile against an API database to isolate exactly what went wrong during my bulk fermentation phase?"
  • Scenario 2: "I want to change my current baking routine to make a loaf with 78% hydration using 450 grams of King Arthur Bread Flour. How can I quickly calculate the exact water and starter weights without doing manual baker's percentage algebra, and save my preferences directly to the device?"

Typography

Heading Typography: Merriweather (Serif)

Applied to major article headings (H1, H2, H3). This font conveys literary reliability, tradition, and academic weight, mirroring the historic and artisanal nature of natural fermentation.

Body Typography: Work Sans (Sans-Serif)

Applied to main prose, dashboard calculations, navigation elements, and layout grids. This highly legible, modern sans-serif ensures data readability across small viewports when reading dense baking directions or calculating weights.
